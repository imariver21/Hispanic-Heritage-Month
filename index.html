Guillermo González Camarena

On February 17, 1917, In Guadalajara, Mexico, Guillermo González Camarena was born, He had 6 siblings, 5 of which were male. He invented "Chromoscopy Adapter for Television Equipment" which was an early form of color television González Camarena applied for this patent August 14, 1940, and obtained the patent September 15, 1942. He sold his first set in 1954 for about $1,450.He died in a car accident in Puebla on April 18, 1965, at the age of 48.

Claudio Castillón Lévano

Claudio Castillón Lévano was born in Peru, and he invented the "Neonatal artificial bubble" which improves the intensive care of high-risk newborns. The purpose of the invention is to improve the intensive care of high-risk newborns, comprising a neonatal capsule hermetically closed to prevent contamination of the child by the external environment. The neonatal capsule is connected to an air feeding means for alimenting the newborn child within the capsule with sterile air.

Elena Nuñez

Elena Nuñez invented a "Banana Flavoring Process", she recieved her patent June 19 1990. Her pantent stated "a process for producing a banana flavored chip includes frying banana slices, coating them in banana puree; quick frying the coated slices; and cooling the quick fried coated slices to yield the flavored banana chips"
